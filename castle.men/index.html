History of Phonk
When started first phonk rap
1994 - Past
Phonk started as an internet genre in the early 2010s, but its roots go back to 1990s Memphis rap. Memphis, 1990s: • Lo-fi sound • Cassette recordings • Dark themes (crime, streets) • Heavy 808 drums Key artists: • Three 6 Mafia • DJ Paul • Juicy J • Tommy Wright III These artists created the foundation for phonk.
Birth of Phonk
Birth - 2010-2013
Birth of Phonk (2010–2013): The term “phonk” appeared online thanks to: • SpaceGhostPurrp • Raider Klan They were inspired by Memphis rap but added: • More reverb • Atmospheric beats • Internet aesthetic This is considered the first “true” phonk — slow, hazy, and gritty. Evolution (2015–2020): Phonk spread through SoundCloud: • Soudiere • DJ Smokey
Later, Drift-phonk
First release - 2011-2012
Later, drift-phonk emerged — faster and more aggressive — popularized on TikTok by producers like DVRST. Summary: There’s no single “first track,” but early SpaceGhostPurrp releases (2011–2012) are considered the start of phonk as a separate genre. Before phonk, it was just Memphis rap. After phonk, the genre evolved online into multiple styles.
